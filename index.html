Research Work

Ongoing and completed research projects in computer architecture and neuromorphic computing.

SNAP-V: A RISC-V SoC with Configurable Neuromorphic Acceleration for Small-Scale Spiking Neural Networks (Final Year Thesis)

Nov. 2024 - Jul. 2025

Final Year Thesis RISC-V SoC Neuromorphic H-NoC Energy-Efficient

Designed and developed a dual-core RISC-V System-on-Chip integrating a configurable neuromorphic accelerator with over 1k LIF neurons organized into parallel clusters interconnected through a hierarchical Network-on-Chip (H-NoC), enabling efficient spike-based computation for small-scale SNNs in low-power edge applications.

Validated the SoC on MNIST using Synopsys (VCS/PrimePower) and Xilinx Vivado, achieving 96.69% accuracy (within an average 2.62% of baseline) and state-of-the-art energy efficiency of 1.39 pJ/synaptic operation.

Supervision: Dr. Isuru Nawinne, Prof. Roshan G. Ragel

Optimized Multi-Processor System-on-Chip (MPSoC) Design for Low-Resource JPEG Encoding

IEEE ICAC 2024 | Dec. 2024

IEEE Conference MPSoC FPGA Custom Instructions Pipeline Optimization

Designed and optimized a Multiprocessor System-on-Chip (MPSoC) architecture for low-resource JPEG encoding using six Nios II/e CPUs on Cyclone IV E FPGA. Implemented a pipelined JPEG encoder with stages for color space conversion, DCT, quantization, and Huffman encoding.

Optimized performance using custom hardware components including FIFO queues, custom instruction blocks, and evaluated superscalar pipelines, achieving 2.8× throughput improvement and significant resource efficiency gains.

Authors: K. Gunawardana, C. Adhikari, I. Nawinne | DOI: 10.1109/ICAC64487.2024.10851123
